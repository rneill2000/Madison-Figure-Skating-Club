Your Skating Journey

From your first glide to center ice — here's how it all unfolds.

Whether you're 4 or 40, everyone starts somewhere. Skating is a lifelong sport, and Madison has everything you need at every stage. Click any step to learn more.

1
The Beginning

Learn to Skate

It all starts here. Madison Ice Inc. offers group classes for every age and level — no experience needed.

Madison Ice Skating School runs Learn to Skate USA classes at Madison Ice Arena and Hartmeyer Ice Arena throughout the year. Programs include:

  • Teddy Bear (Ages 2–4) — A parent is on the ice with the child. Learning through play, songs, and games.
  • Snowplow Sam 1–4 (Ages 4–6) — Fun, comfort, and movement on ice. Games and toys make it exciting.
  • Basic 1–6 (Ages 6–13) — Proper technique, safety, and progressive skill building from gliding to crossovers.
  • Adult Beginner 1–3 — It's never too late! Classes designed for adults learning to skate for the first time.

Sessions run in 6–8 week blocks, with multiple sessions offered per year. Skaters progress by passing required elements at each level.

2
Level Up

Build Your Skills

After basics, you'll move into Free Skate levels where real figure skating skills come alive — jumps, spins, and footwork.

Once you've completed the Basic levels, it's time for the Free Skate curriculum — this is where figure skating really begins:

  • Pre Free Skate — Introduction to edges, turns, and the fundamentals of free skating.
  • Free Skate 1–6 — Each level covers three areas: skating skills, jumps, and spins. You'll learn waltz jumps, toe loops, salchows, and basic spins.
  • Hockey Track (1–4) — If hockey is your goal, there's a dedicated skills progression too.

This is also when many skaters start thinking about private coaching to accelerate their progress.

3
Go Further

Find a Private Coach

Ready to take things to the next level? A private coach gives you personalized attention and helps you set real goals.

Private lessons are where skaters make the biggest leaps. FSCM has 15 certified coaches with a range of specialties. Here's how to get started:

  • Watch and ask — Observe coaches during club sessions. Ask other skaters and parents for recommendations.
  • Try a lesson — Most coaches offer trial lessons so you can find the right fit for your learning style.
  • Consider your goals — Some coaches specialize in freestyle and jumps, others in ice dance, moves in the field, or choreography.
  • Credentials matter — All FSCM coaches maintain U.S. Figure Skating certification and Coach I.C.E. compliance.

Private lessons are typically 20–30 minutes and take place during club freestyle sessions. Costs vary by coach.

4
Become Part of the Family

Join the Club

Joining FSCM gives you access to club ice, competitions, test sessions, ice shows, and a welcoming skating community.

FSCM membership unlocks everything the club has to offer:

  • 500+ hours of club ice at Madison Ice Arena and Hartmeyer Ice Arena
  • U.S. Figure Skating membership included — required for testing and competing
  • Test sessions to officially track your progress through the USFS level structure
  • Competition eligibility for USFS-sanctioned events
  • Ice show participation — perform in our annual show!
  • Community — social events, service projects, and lifelong friendships

5
Open to Everyone

Perform in Ice Shows

Our annual ice show is one of the highlights of the year — and it's open to skaters of ALL levels, from beginners to advanced.

You don't have to be a competitive skater to perform! Ice shows are for everyone in the club:

  • Group numbers — Skate with others at your level in themed choreography
  • Solo & duet opportunities — For more advanced skaters who want to showcase their skills
  • Costumes & music — Each year has a fun theme (this year: Blades on Broadway!)
  • A real audience — Family, friends, and the Madison community come out to watch

For many skaters, the ice show is their favorite part of the year. It's a chance to perform, build confidence, and celebrate all the hard work.

6
Test Your Skills

Start Competing

When you're ready, competitions are an incredible way to challenge yourself, set goals, and see how you measure up.

There are several competition pathways, and your coach will help you decide when you're ready:

  • Compete USA — The perfect entry point. Low-pressure, fun, and designed for beginners. FSCM hosts the Badger Classic in this format.
  • Excel Series — More advanced competitions with structured levels, but still balanced for skaters who aren't on the full competitive track.
  • Championship/Qualifying — The national qualifying track for skaters aiming for Regionals, Sectionals, and beyond.

What you need to compete:

  • U.S. Figure Skating membership (included with FSCM membership)
  • Appropriate test level passed for your competition level
  • A program with music, choreographed with your coach
  • Competition attire that meets USFS guidelines

FSCM hosts USFS test sessions on the 14th and last day of each month — both virtual and in-person.

7
Skate as a Team

Join a Synchro Team

Love skating with others? Synchronized skating teams combine figure skating with teamwork, formations, and shared goals.

Madison is home to the Madison Ice Diamonds, with teams at multiple levels:

  • Aspire Snowplow Sam — For the youngest skaters just starting out
  • Aspire Beginner & Pre-Preliminary — Building skills and learning formations
  • Pre-Juvenile & Open Juvenile — Competitive synchro at regional and national events
  • Open Adult/Masters — Yes, adults can do synchro too!

There's also an Intro to Synchronized Skating class through Madison Ice Inc. where you can try it out before committing to a team. Synchro teaches teamwork, discipline, and creates incredible friendships.

8
The Journey Continues

Keep Growing

Skating is a lifelong sport. Whether you're chasing gold or just chasing joy on the ice, there's always another goal ahead.

The beauty of figure skating is that it never ends. As you progress, you can:

  • Pass higher USFS tests — Work your way through Pre-Preliminary, Preliminary, Pre-Juvenile, Juvenile, Intermediate, Novice, Junior, and Senior levels
  • Compete in adult events — FSCM hosts the Midwestern Adult Sectional Championships
  • Give back — Help with the club's community service projects, mentor younger skaters, or volunteer at competitions
  • Become a coach — Many of our coaches started right here in Madison

Whatever your goals, FSCM is here to support you every step of the way.
